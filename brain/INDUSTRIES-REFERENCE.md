# ProfitPulse Industries Reference & Awareness Calendar

 This file defines the industries ProfitPulse serves and the awareness days worth tying content to. Use it when producing industry-focused posts (twice per week).

## Industries served

Group the industries by cluster when thinking about content angles. The clusters share commercial pressures even when the surface businesses look different.

### Healthcare and allied services
- Allied health and medical practices (physio, psychology, podiatry, OT, speech)
- Dental and specialist clinics
- Veterinary practices
- Aged care and disability (NDIS) providers

Commercial pressures common to this cluster: regulatory compliance load, clinician burnout and recruitment, payor mix shifts (Medicare, private health, NDIS), owner-dependent valuation, succession planning timelines.

### Professional services
- Professional services (law, accounting)
- Engineering and architecture consultancies
- Management and IT consulting
- Marketing, creative and media agencies
- Recruitment and labour hire

Commercial pressures: utilisation versus realisation, scope creep, partner economics, capacity ceilings, talent retention, AI disruption to billable hours.

### Property and construction
- Real estate agencies and property management
- Property development and construction
- Building and specialist trades

Commercial pressures: project cash flow timing, working capital intensity, fixed price risk, subcontractor management, insurance and licensing overhead.

### Goods and manufacturing
- Manufacturing
- Food and beverage production
- Wholesale and distribution
- Importers, exporters and customs brokers
- Mining services contractors

Commercial pressures: inventory carrying cost, FX exposure, supplier concentration, freight inflation, customer payment terms.

### Movement and trade
- Transport, logistics and freight

Commercial pressures: fuel cost volatility, fleet financing, driver shortage, fixed-route economics, scheduling rhythms.

### Customer-facing retail and hospitality
- Retail (bricks and mortar)
- E-commerce and online retail
- Hospitality (cafes, restaurants, bars)
- Accommodation and tourism operators

Commercial pressures: foot traffic, gross margin compression, wage costs versus trading hours, seasonality, online versus physical channel economics.

### Education and care
- Childcare and education providers
- Private schools and registered training organisations

Commercial pressures: regulatory compliance, government funding rhythms, staff-to-child ratios, enrolment cycles, capital-heavy fit-outs.

### Wellbeing and personal services
- Fitness, wellness and personal services

Commercial pressures: membership churn, capacity per session, owner-operator dependency, lease economics.

### Land-based and primary
- Agriculture and primary production

Commercial pressures: seasonality, weather risk, commodity price exposure, capital intensity, succession.

### Technology and modern operators
- Technology and SaaS
- Online course creators, coaches and paid communities
- Biotech, medtech and life sciences scaleups
- Renewable energy installers (solar, battery, EV)

Commercial pressures: customer acquisition cost, unit economics, runway and burn, capital raising cycles, gross margin discipline.

### Mission-driven and specialised
- Not-for-profits with commercial arms
- Indigenous-owned enterprises and social enterprises
- Professional sporting clubs and academies
- Family offices and private investment vehicles

Commercial pressures: dual mission, grant and sponsorship cycles, governance, restricted versus unrestricted income, succession.

### Franchise structures
- Franchise systems (franchisors)
- Multi-site franchisees

Commercial pressures: royalty and marketing fund economics, site-by-site performance variance, capital allocation across locations, brand consistency.

## Awareness day calendar

When today's date matches one of these dates (or the same week, where flexibility makes sense), prioritise the linked industry cluster for that day's industry-focused post. Tie-in should be light: one sentence opening reference at most, then a substantive commercial post.

## How to use this file

When the user requests today's draft and asks for industry-focused content, or when today's date sits on an awareness day, scan the calendar above and the industry cluster it points to. Pick a specific industry inside the cluster (not the whole cluster) for sharper commercial substance.

When producing the post, use the industry's own vocabulary, name the industry plainly in the title or opening paragraph, and ensure the FAQs and internal links all carry the same industry frame. Cross-reference the PRODUCTS-REFERENCE.md to match the industry to one or two ProfitPulse services that genuinely fit its pattern of issues.

Industry-focused posts should still hold up as substantive commercial reads even for readers outside that industry. The aim is depth, not just a hat tip.
